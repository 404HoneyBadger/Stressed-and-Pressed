Existential Dread

I really don't like writing about myself. So I will quote one of my WIN application submissions. If I have to be uncomfortable, so do you.

Id like to talk about my most recent hardship of surviving suicide loss while organizing a Latin Heratige Festival. The immense grief and pain I had experienced the three months following his passing was a catalyst to create an inclusive event that recognised, validated and celebrated the Latin community, our community. I dove deep into researching the voting, economic and social impact of the Latin community, taught myself how to use adobe illustrator to create a professional sponsorship proposal and reached out to every municipality in Gaston county. Every hurdle was a challenge to overcome my limitations, whether it was community outreach in a language I wasn't fluent in or finding the funding for the festival in a county that notoriously disenfranchised their minority demographic, giving up was not an option. This experience shifted my perception of what I was capable of and how I process and navigate both personal and professional challenges.
